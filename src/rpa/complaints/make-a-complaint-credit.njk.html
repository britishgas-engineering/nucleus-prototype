Know your rights

You can get free, independent advice to know your rights as an energy consumer. It will help you find other deals, tell you how to complain, get advice about the quality of your electricity or gas supply, or ask for help if you’re struggling to pay your bills.

For help on where you can get free independent information and advice about your energy supply and ways to reduce your energy bills, download the Know your rights (PDF 96.8KB) from Citizens Advice.

We always aim to resolve complaints as quickly as possible

We'll make every effort to resolve your complaint within a day of receiving it. However, sometimes it can take a little longer. If we haven't been able to sort things out within eight weeks, or if we can't agree a way forward with you (known as a 'deadlock'), we'll write and let you know that you have the right to complain the Ombudsman Services: Energy.

The Ombudsman service is there to help sort out disputes between energy suppliers and their customers. It’s free and they’re totally independent – they don’t take sides and their decisions are based only on the information they have.

You don’t have to accept their decision, but if you do, we’ll act on what they say. That might mean saying sorry, explaining what’s gone wrong, fixing the problem or paying you compensation.

You'll find the Ombudsman’s factsheet, which gives you full details of their services and how they can help, by contacting them in the following ways:

Telephone: 0330 440 1624 Monday to Friday 9-5pm.
Textphone: 0330 440 1600 Monday to Friday 9-5pm.
Website: www.ombudsman-services.org/energy
Email: enquiry@ombudsman-services.org
